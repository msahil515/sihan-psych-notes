# Ch 9: Abraham Maslow: Needs-Hierarchy Theory

Maslow, founder of humanistic psychology, proposed a hierarchy of five innate needs culminating in **self-actualization** — the fullest realization of potential. Unlike Freud (pathology) or behaviorists (observable behavior), he studied the healthiest, most fulfilled people. His theory is optimistic and rooted in the belief that human nature is fundamentally good.

---

## The Life of Maslow (1908–1970)

### Childhood
- Born **1908** in **Brooklyn**; **oldest of seven children**; poor immigrant parents.
- "With my childhood, it's a wonder I'm not psychotic." Called his family "miserable," his mother "a horrible creature" who **never showed affection** and killed his stray kittens. He refused to attend her funeral: "The whole thrust of my life-philosophy...has its roots in a hatred...against everything she stood for."
- Felt ugly and inferior (father mocked his appearance); compensated through **athletic achievement** — a living example of **Adler's compensation**.

### Education and Career
- Married his cousin **Bertha** at the **University of Wisconsin**, which gave his life direction.
- Became enraptured with **John B. Watson's behaviorism**; did experimental work on **dominance and sex in primates**.
- Two shifts away from behaviorism: **WWII** and the **birth of his first child** ("anyone who had a baby couldn't be a behaviorist").
- **Ph.D., Wisconsin, 1934**; postdoc under **E. L. Thorndike** at Columbia (IQ tested at **195**). Met emigrant intellectuals **Karen Horney** and **Alfred Adler**, plus **Max Wertheimer** and anthropologist **Ruth Benedict** — sparking his self-actualization ideas.
- **1941**: a post-Pearl Harbor parade resolved him to study the highest human ideals. Taught at **Brandeis (1951–1969)**; **APA president, 1967**. Died **1970** of a heart attack while jogging.

## The Hierarchy of Needs

**Five innate needs** (1968, 1970b), bottom to top:
1. Physiological → 2. Safety → 3. Belongingness and Love → 4. Esteem → 5. Self-Actualization

### Key Terminology
- **Hierarchy of five innate needs**: arrangement from strongest (bottom) to weakest (top) that activates and directs behavior.
- **Instinctoid needs**: innate (hereditary component), but the **behaviors to satisfy them are learned**.
- **Lower needs must be at least partially satisfied** before higher needs become influential. Only **one need dominates** at a time. The hierarchy can be **reversed** by circumstances (e.g., recession).

### Characteristics of Needs
1. **Strength inversely related to position**: lower = stronger/more urgent.
2. **Higher needs appear later**: physiological/safety (infancy), belongingness/esteem (adolescence), self-actualization (midlife).
3. **Higher needs can be postponed**: failing a higher need does not produce a crisis; failing a lower need **does**.
4. **Deficit/Deficiency needs (D-needs)**: the lower needs.
5. **Growth/Being needs (B-needs)**: the higher needs; satisfaction → growth, health, fulfillment.
6. **Partial satisfaction**: a need need not be fully met before the next emerges (Maslow's example: physiological 85%, safety 70%, belongingness 50%, esteem 40%, self-actualization 10%).

### Physiological Needs
- Most basic/powerful: food, water, **sex** (sex is physiological, not a love need). A gratified need no longer motivates.

### Safety Needs
- Stability, security, order, freedom from fear. Most visible in **infants/children** (preference for structure/routine). **Neurotic, insecure adults** remain dominated by safety needs. Important for infants and neurotics, not overwhelmingly for healthy adults.

### Belongingness and Love Needs
- Close relationships or group membership (including social media). **Love ≠ sex** (sex is physiological). Failure to satisfy love → a fundamental cause of emotional maladjustment.

### Esteem Needs
- **Self-esteem** (self-worth) and **esteem from others** (status, recognition). Satisfaction → confidence, competence, productivity; lack → inferiority, helplessness.

### The Self-Actualization Need
- The **highest need**: fullest development and realization of potential. Failure → restlessness, frustration. "A musician must make music...to be ultimately at peace." Not limited to elites: "A first-rate soup is more creative than a second-rate painting."

**Conditions for self-actualization:**
1. Free of societal and self-imposed constraints.
2. Not distracted by lower-order needs.
3. Secure in self-image and relationships; able to love and be loved.
4. Realistic knowledge of strengths and weaknesses.

**Non-traditional routes**: people who fast to death for beliefs, religious figures abandoning worldly goods, artists imperiling health — denying lower needs for self-actualization. Common reversal: esteem above love.

### Cognitive Needs
- A second set of innate needs: to **know** and to **understand** — **outside** the five-need hierarchy.
- The **need to know is stronger** than the need to understand. Appear in late infancy as curiosity; don't need teaching but can be inhibited. **Impossible to self-actualize** without satisfying them.
- Evidence: animals explore for no reward; people seek knowledge at risk of life; healthy adults are drawn to the mysterious and bored by "stupid jobs."

## The Study of Self-Actualizers

### Metamotivation
- **Metamotivation (B-motivation/Being-motivation)**: self-actualizers maximize **potential** rather than striving for a specific goal (meta- = "beyond"). They develop **from within**.

| Feature | D-Motivation | B-Motivation (Metamotivation) |
|---|---|---|
| Who | Non-self-actualizers | Self-actualizers |
| Nature | Striving to make up for a lack | Developing from within |
| Goal | Specific goal object | No specific goal |
| Tension | Seeks to **reduce** | Seeks to **increase** (challenge) |

- D-motivation applies to physiological, safety, love, esteem needs; self-actualizers are in a state of **"being."**

### Metaneeds and Metapathology
- **Metaneeds**: states of growth/being toward which self-actualizers evolve (truth, goodness, beauty, justice, order). Failure → **metapathology** (thwarted self-development; feeling helpless/depressed without identifiable cause).

| Metaneed | Metapathology |
|---|---|
| Truth | Mistrust, cynicism |
| Goodness | Hatred, disgust |
| Beauty | Vulgarity, restlessness |
| Unity/Wholeness | Disintegration |
| Aliveness | Deadness, loss of zest |
| Uniqueness | Loss of individuality |
| Perfection | Hopelessness |
| Justice | Anger, lawlessness |
| Order | Insecurity |
| Meaningfulness | Despair, senselessness |

*(Adapted from The Farther Reaches of Human Nature, Maslow, 1971.)*

### Characteristics of Self-Actualizers
Constitute **1% or less** of the population; first observed in **Ruth Benedict** and **Max Wertheimer**.

| Characteristic | Description |
|---|---|
| **Efficient perception of reality** | See the world clearly, without prejudgment |
| **Acceptance** of self, others, nature | Without distortion or guilt |
| **Spontaneity, simplicity, naturalness** | Open, direct; rarely hide feelings |
| **Focus on problems outside themselves** | Mission/vocation — a **requirement** for self-actualization |
| **Detachment, need for privacy** | Autonomous; need solitude |
| **Freshness of appreciation** | Wonder and awe at recurring experiences |
| **Peak experiences** | Moments of ecstasy; the self is transcended |
| **Social interest** (from Adler) | Empathy for all humanity |
| **Profound interpersonal relations** | Few but deep friendships |
| **Creativeness** | Inventive, flexible, willing to err |
| **Resistance to enculturation** | Independent; resist social pressure |

- Self-actualizers still have **human flaws** (can be rude/ruthless), but less often than others.

### Failure to Become Self-Actualizing
- Self-actualization is the **weakest need** (highest position) → easily inhibited; <1% reach it.
- **Reasons**: (1) **hostile/rejecting parents** (impair love/esteem); (2) **poor economic conditions** (lower needs dominate); (3) **inadequate education/child-rearing** (sex-role training, overprotection, OR excessive permissiveness; ideal = permissiveness within limits, with guidance); (4) **childhood deficits in love/security** (sufficient love in the first two years is prerequisite; compare **Erikson's** trust and **Horney's** security).

#### The Jonah Complex
- **Jonah complex**: fear that maximizing potential will create an unmanageable situation (biblical Jonah, who fled his calling). We both fear and are thrilled by our possibilities, but fear often wins. Self-actualization requires **courage**; self-actualizers constantly test themselves by abandoning secure routines.

## Questions about Human Nature

| Dimension | Maslow |
|---|---|
| Free will vs. determinism | Strong **free will**; responsible for our development |
| Nature vs. nurture | **Interaction**: needs innate, satisfying behaviors learned |
| Uniqueness | Emphasis on **uniqueness** (universal needs, varied behaviors) |
| Early vs. later | Childhood important but we are **not victims** |
| Optimism | **Optimistic, humanistic** |
| Human nature | Basically **good**; evil results from a poor environment, not heredity |

## Assessment in Maslow's Theory

- Began as **personal curiosity** about Benedict and Wertheimer. First subjects: **college students** — only **1 in 3,000** self-actualizing. Moved to middle-aged/older; still <1%.
- Historical figures studied: **Jefferson, Einstein, George Washington Carver, Harriet Tubman, Eleanor Roosevelt**.
- Methods: biographical analysis, interviews, free association, projective tests, **indirect observation** (subjects were self-conscious). Maslow admitted: "By ordinary standards of laboratory research, this simply was not research at all" — he saw it as **pilot studies**.

### The Personal Orientation Inventory (POI)
- **Everett Shostrom** (1964, 1974): **150 paired statements**; measures self-actualization. Two major scales:
  - **Time competence**: living **in the present**.
  - **Inner directedness**: depending on **oneself** for judgments/values.

### The Smartphone Basic Needs Scale
- **Kang & Jung (2014)**: **20-item** inventory assessing how smartphone use satisfies Maslow's needs (US and South Korean students); high validity/reliability.

## Research on Maslow's Theory

### The Hierarchy of Needs
> **Williams & Page (1989)**: satisfying safety, belongingness, esteem needs was **negatively related to neuroticism/depression**; esteem stronger than belongingness in college students.
> **Lester (1990)**: higher need satisfaction = **lower neuroticism** (EPI).
> **Graham & Balloun (1973)**: supported the **order** of the five needs; concern increased from lowest to highest.
> **Noltemeyer et al. (2012)**: disadvantaged youth focused on **safety needs**.
> **Tay & Diener (2011)**: 40,000+ people, **123 countries** — basic needs must be met before fulfillment/happiness.
> **Taormina & Gao (2013); Ivtzan et al. (2013)**: more basic needs met → more higher needs met; people **over 36** more likely to self-actualize.

### The Belongingness Need
> **Gardner, Pickett, & Brewer (2000)**: students whose belongingness was **thwarted** (chat-room exclusion) recalled **more social events** from a diary.
> **Smith & Williams (2004)**: chat-room exclusion lowered self-esteem, control, belongingness within **8 minutes**.
> **Begen & Turner-Cobb (2012)**: teens with a sense of **belonging** were **physically healthier**.

### Self-Esteem
- **High self-esteem**: more job offers (Ellis & Taylor, 1983); copes better with job loss (Shamir, 1986); higher perceived skills (Campbell et al., 2002); more school sports, lower anxiety (Bowker, 2006). **Unstable** self-esteem predicted desire for fame (Noser & Zeigler-Hill, 2014).
- **Low self-esteem** (multinational): linked to anxiety, depression, smoking, dropout, criminal convictions, financial problems, more physical health problems, more death anxiety; more **time on Facebook** (Kalpidou et al., 2011). Students valued self-esteem boosts over food, alcohol, sex, or money (Bushman et al., 2011). Appearance determined self-esteem for >1/3 of adolescents, no gender differences (Seidah & Bouffard, 2007).
- **Politics**: among older people, conservative beliefs ↔ high self-esteem (Van Hiel & Brebels, 2011); voters preferred candidates with **higher self-esteem** (Ziegler-Hill & Myers, 2009).
- **Lifespan**: self-esteem **rises** through adolescence/adulthood, **peaks ~age 60**, then **declines**; changes little after **age 30** (Orth & Robins, 2014; Huang, 2010b).
- **Culture**: Black teenagers report **higher** self-esteem than White; **Japanese** students consistently score **lowest** (50+ countries; Schmitt & Allik, 2005); Asian Americans lower than European Americans; stronger ethnic identity ↔ higher self-esteem among immigrants (Verkuyten, 2009; Lopez, 2008).

### Self-Actualization (POI)
- Higher POI scores positively related to emotional health, creativity, well-being after therapy, autonomy, racial tolerance; negatively to alcoholism, neuroticism, depression. Self-actualization develops **gradually over the lifespan**.

### Peak Experiences
> **Hoffman, Kaneshiro, & Compton (2012)** (Americans 40–65): top three = joyful experiences with **others**, a **sense of achievement**, **personal growth**. Cross-culturally: mainland Chinese reported more **serenity**; Brazil/Portugal more **developmental landmarks**.

## Self-Determination Theory

- **SDT** (Edward **Deci** and Richard **Ryan**): three **basic psychological needs** for well-being:
  1. **Autonomy** — actions self-chosen.
  2. **Competence** — feeling effective.
  3. **Relatedness** — feeling connected.
- **Intrinsic motivation**: doing an activity for its own sake; **extrinsic**: for external reward. Intrinsic motivation is supported when autonomy/competence are met.
- **Overjustification effect**: external rewards for intrinsically motivated activity can **decrease** intrinsic motivation. SDT resonates with Maslow's **metamotivation**; autonomy-supportive environments foster well-being.

## Reflections on Maslow's Theory

### Contributions
- **Founded humanistic psychology**; shifted focus from pathology to **health and potential**.
- The **hierarchy of needs** is enormously influential (psychology, education, business/management).
- Pioneered the study of **positive characteristics** (precursor to positive psychology); introduced **peak experiences, metaneeds, metamotivation, Jonah complex**.

### Criticisms
- Admitted **lack of scientific rigor** (no controls, sampling, blind assessment).
- **Circular reasoning** (self-actualizers defined by their traits, used as evidence).
- **Sampling bias**; **vague definitions**; **cultural bias** (Western/individualistic); the strict **order** of the hierarchy is not always confirmed; **elitist** (<1%); inadequate developmental account.

## Chapter Summary

- Maslow rejected behaviorism/psychoanalysis to study the healthiest individuals.
- **Hierarchy of five instinctoid needs** (strongest → weakest): physiological, safety, belongingness/love, esteem, self-actualization; lower (deficit) needs precede higher (growth) needs.
- Also proposed **cognitive needs** (know/understand) outside the hierarchy.
- **Self-actualizers** (<1%) show ~12 traits (efficient perception, acceptance, spontaneity, social interest, peak experiences, creativeness, resistance to enculturation).
- They operate via **metamotivation (B-motivation)**, evolve toward **metaneeds**, and suffer **metapathology** when unmet.
- Failure to self-actualize: weak highest need, hostile parents, poor economics, improper child-rearing, the **Jonah complex**.
- Tools: **POI (Shostrom, 1964)**, **Smartphone Basic Needs Scale (Kang & Jung, 2014)**.
- **SDT** (Deci & Ryan) extends Maslow with autonomy, competence, relatedness.

## Likely Exam Points

1. **Hierarchy (bottom to top)**: Physiological → Safety → Belongingness/Love → Esteem → Self-Actualization; lower needs stronger and partially satisfied first.

2. **Instinctoid needs**: innate, but satisfying behaviors are **learned**.

3. **Deficit vs. Growth needs**: failing a deficit need causes a crisis; failing a growth need impairs development but no crisis.

4. **Cognitive needs** (know/understand) are **outside** the hierarchy; the need to know is **stronger**.

5. **Metamotivation (B-motivation)** — develop from within, **increase tension**; vs. **D-motivation** (deficiency).

6. **Metaneeds** (truth, beauty, justice, order); unmet → **metapathology**.

7. **Self-actualizers** = **<1%**; Maslow found 1 in 3,000 college students.

8. **Peak experience**: ecstasy where the self is transcended.

9. **Jonah complex**: fear of one's own greatness (biblical Jonah).

10. **POI** (Shostrom, 1964): 150 forced-choice items; scales = **time competence** and **inner directedness**.

11. **Social interest** borrowed from **Adler**.

12. **Human nature**: free will, optimistic, basically good; evil is environmental.

13. **SDT** (Deci & Ryan): **autonomy, competence, relatedness**.

14. **Tay & Diener (2011)**: 40,000+, 123 countries — basic needs precede happiness.

15. **Conditions for self-actualization**: free of constraints, not distracted by lower needs, secure in self/relationships, realistic self-knowledge.
